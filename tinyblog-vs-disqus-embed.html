Comparison · TinyBlog vs third-party embed widgets

A self-hosted embed with no trackers on your readers.

Third-party embed widgets like Disqus are convenient, but many load cross-site cookies, tracking scripts, and ads onto your page. TinyBlog's one-line embed serves its own JSON from your server — same drop-in convenience, none of the third-party baggage.

Context · 01

What third-party embeds do.

Hosted embed widgets — Disqus is the best-known example — let you add functionality with a snippet of script. The trade is that the script, the data, and often advertising and analytics live on the vendor's infrastructure. Disqus in particular is a comment and community platform whose free tier has historically served ads and loaded third-party trackers, and whose content is stored in the vendor cloud rather than on your site.

Context · 02

How TinyBlog differs.

TinyBlog gives you the same one-line convenience with the opposite data model. The widget is a small, dependency-free script that talks only to a JSON API on your own PHP host, guarded by a CORS origin allowlist. There are no third-party cookies, no ad networks, and no analytics beacons — just your posts, served from your server, sanitized on the way in and on the way out. Note the scope difference: Disqus is primarily comments, while TinyBlog embeds your blog feed, a single post, or a subscribe box.

Decision

Which should you choose?

Choose a third-party embed if…

you specifically need hosted comments or community discussion with moderation tooling out of the box, and you're comfortable with the vendor hosting that data and running their scripts on your pages.

Choose TinyBlog if…

you want to embed your own blog content — a feed, a post, or a subscribe box — with the same one-line simplicity but no third-party trackers, no ads, and all data on infrastructure you control.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Does the TinyBlog widget load third-party trackers?

No. The TinyBlog embed is a single dependency-free script that fetches JSON only from your own endpoint. There are no third-party cookies, no advertising scripts, and no cross-site tracking — the data path is your host to your reader.

Is TinyBlog a Disqus alternative?

For the embeddable-content use case, yes. Disqus is a hosted third-party widget best known for comments; its free tier has historically included ads and tracking. TinyBlog covers the self-hosted, privacy-first side of embedding — a blog feed, post, or subscribe box you fully control. It is not a comment system.

Where does the embedded data come from?

From your PHP host. The widget calls a CORS-allowlisted JSON API served by tinyblog.php, so posts and subscriber submissions stay in your own hosting account rather than a vendor cloud.

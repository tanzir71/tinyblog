Context · 01

What Substack is.

Substack is a hosted publishing platform built around email newsletters. It handles hosting, email delivery, payments, and a built-in discovery network, and it is free to start — Substack instead takes a percentage of any paid-subscription revenue. Your publication lives on a Substack subdomain by default, with custom domains available as a paid add-on.

Context · 02

How TinyBlog differs.

TinyBlog takes the opposite trade. There is no platform, no network, and no payment processing — just a single PHP file plus SQLite that you drop onto hosting you already own. It renders canonical post pages, RSS, and an embeddable feed for any site. You give up Substack's built-in email delivery and discovery in exchange for full ownership: your content, your domain, your data, zero fees, and code you can read end to end.

Decision

Which should you choose?

Choose Substack if…

email delivery and a built-in audience network are the point — you want to send newsletters, take payments, and tap discovery without running any infrastructure, and you accept a revenue share and platform hosting in return.

Choose TinyBlog if…

you want a blog you fully own on your own domain — for a store, portfolio, docs, or product updates — with no fees, no lock-in, and a readable codebase, and you're fine bringing your own email tool if you need one.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Is TinyBlog a free alternative to Substack?

Yes. TinyBlog is MIT-licensed and free for commercial use. It has no per-subscriber fees and takes no cut of paid subscriptions, because it never handles payments — you run it on hosting you already pay for.

Can I move my content off Substack to TinyBlog?

Substack lets you export posts and your subscriber list. TinyBlog stores posts as Markdown in a SQLite database on your own server, so once imported your archive lives on your domain with no platform lock-in.

Does TinyBlog send newsletters like Substack?

TinyBlog captures subscribers and publishes a blog feed and RSS, but it is not a full email-sending platform. If email delivery is central, pair TinyBlog's subscriber capture with your own mailer, or keep Substack for email.

Comparison · TinyBlog vs DropInBlog

TinyBlog vs DropInBlog: the $0 self-hosted embed.

DropInBlog is a polished managed blog product with support, hosted infrastructure, and ecommerce integrations from $49/mo. TinyBlog is the smaller choice: one PHP file, SQLite, and a one-line widget you host yourself with no trackers.

Read the docs Back to overview

Context · 01

What DropInBlog is.

DropInBlog sells managed convenience: a hosted blog engine, editor, SEO tools, integrations, support, and team plans. Its official pricing currently starts at $49/mo for the Solo monthly plan, with higher tiers for more users and business features. That can be the right trade if you want a vendor to run the product for you.

Context · 02

How TinyBlog differs.

TinyBlog is not managed SaaS. It is $0 self-hosted software: upload one PHP file, keep the SQLite database on your host, and embed the feed with a dependency-free script. You trade vendor support and managed convenience for ownership, inspectable source, no trackers, and no monthly product bill.

Decision

Which should you choose?

Choose DropInBlog if...

you want a hosted product with vendor support, ecommerce integrations, SEO tooling, and team workflows, and the monthly SaaS cost is easier than maintaining even a tiny self-hosted tool.

Choose TinyBlog if...

you want the blog embed job without a subscription: one readable backend file, one widget file, your data on your host, and no trackers on readers.
